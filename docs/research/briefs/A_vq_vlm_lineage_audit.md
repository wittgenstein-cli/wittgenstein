# Brief A — VQ / VLM Lineage Audit

**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Author:** research (max.zhuang.yan@gmail.com)
**Status:** Draft v0.1
**Summary:** Audits whether Wittgenstein's "LLM plans in symbols → trained adapter → frozen pretrained VQ decoder → pixels" bet still holds in April 2026 against Chameleon, MAGVIT-v2 / LFQ, Emu3, SPAE, DeepSeek-VL2, and TA-TiTok. Verdict: bet still right, with one forced revision to the word "codebook."

---

## Context

Wittgenstein's master thesis is that a frontier LLM should stay text-native, and every non-text modality should be grafted on via a harness: small trained adapters plus rule-based codecs. For images specifically, the bet is: the LLM emits a symbolic plan, a trained adapter maps that plan to a sequence of discrete tokens, and a frozen off-the-shelf VQ decoder renders pixels. That design only makes sense if (a) discrete image tokens remain a credible interface between LLMs and pixels, and (b) the right decoder for 2026 is a pretrained VQ family model rather than a diffusion UNet, a native autoregressive transformer stack, or something that didn't exist when ADR-0005 was written. This brief is a sanity check on those two assumptions before we freeze more of the image path.

## Steelman

State it as its proponents would.

The discrete-token interface has been the only interface to survive every generation of scale. VQ-VAE (van den Oord et al., 2017, arXiv:1711.00937) established that a codebook bottleneck produces a compressed, reconstructable representation that is indexable by integers — i.e., shaped exactly like a language-model output layer. VQGAN (Esser et al., 2021, arXiv:2012.09841) added adversarial + perceptual losses on the decoder and made the reconstructions sharp enough to matter at 256² and above. DALL-E 1 (Ramesh et al., 2021, arXiv:2102.12092) proved at scale that a vanilla autoregressive transformer, trained on concatenated text + 1,024 dVAE image tokens, learns cross-modal alignment with no alignment-specific loss. LlamaGen (Sun et al., 2024, arXiv:2406.06525) then showed that a standard LLaMA-architecture transformer on VQ tokens reaches FID 2.18 on ImageNet 256² class-conditional — competitive with DiT-XL/2 — using nothing diffusion-shaped. SEED-LLaMA (Ge et al., 2023, arXiv:2310.01218) showed the tokenizer can be trained to semantically align with language so the same backbone does understanding and generation. TiTok (Yu et al., 2024, arXiv:2406.07550) compressed a 256² image to 32 tokens with rFID 1.97, collapsing the sequence-length cost. FSQ (Mentzer et al., 2023, arXiv:2309.15505) removed the codebook-collapse failure mode of VQ while preserving the discrete interface. And LLaVA (Liu et al., 2023, arXiv:2304.08485) showed the dual direction — a small trained projector is sufficient to bolt a frozen vision encoder onto a frozen LLM for understanding.

The shape of that lineage is what Wittgenstein is betting on. Every paper above treats "discrete indices into a learned or fixed codebook" as the handoff between the language model and pixel space. None of them require the LLM to learn pixel-domain inductive biases. The adapter is trainable; the decoder is frozen; the LLM is untouched. That is exactly the harness model.

## 2026 additions

Six pieces of work that were not in the existing dossier. Details below were confirmed against the arXiv listings.

**Chameleon (Meta FAIR, 2024, arXiv:2405.09818).** "Mixed-Modal Early-Fusion Foundation Models." Chameleon trains a single transformer from scratch on interleaved image + text token streams, with images tokenized by a VQ-family tokenizer to roughly 1,024 tokens per 512² image. It reports state-of-the-art image captioning, competitive text-only performance against Mixtral 8x7B and Gemini-Pro, and non-trivial image generation out of one unified model. Relevance to Wittgenstein: Chameleon is the strongest public evidence that the discrete-token interface survives at frontier scale with no diffusion anywhere in the stack. It argues _for_ the VQ interface and _against_ the "text-native LLM, frozen decoder" split — it shows you can fuse the two, if you are willing to retrain the backbone. Wittgenstein is not willing to. But Chameleon's existence means our decoder choice is defensible: if Meta's frontier team thinks VQ tokens are a reasonable target vocabulary for a 34B-parameter transformer, they are a reasonable target for our adapter.

**MAGVIT-v2 / LFQ (Yu et al., 2023, arXiv:2310.05737).** "Language Model Beats Diffusion — Tokenizer is Key to Visual Generation" (ICLR 2024). Introduces Lookup-Free Quantization: each latent dimension is independently binarized (or low-bit quantized), so the effective codebook is 2^d without any nearest-neighbor lookup or learned embedding table. LFQ enables vocabularies on the order of 2^18 without collapse, and the paper shows that equipped with this tokenizer, LMs outperform diffusion on ImageNet and Kinetics video benchmarks. Relevance: LFQ is the first quantization scheme that is simultaneously (1) discrete enough to be an LM target, (2) large-vocabulary enough to match diffusion's fidelity, and (3) free of the codebook-collapse training pathology. If Wittgenstein picks a modern frozen decoder in 2026, the candidate is almost certainly MAGVIT-v2 or a direct descendant, not a vanilla VQGAN. This is the single most important update to ADR-0005: the decoder family we are betting on is LFQ, not classical VQ.

**Emu3 (BAAI, 2024, arXiv:2409.18869).** "Next-Token Prediction is All You Need." A single transformer trained from scratch on discretized image, video, and text token sequences. Emu3 reports surpassing SDXL on image generation and LLaVA-1.6 on vision-language understanding, _and_ generates coherent video by next-token prediction on video tokens — with no CLIP encoder and no pretrained LLM backbone to start from. Relevance: Emu3 is the most aggressive version of the thesis Chameleon hints at — that with the right discrete tokenizer, you do not need a separate harness at all. It is the strongest argument _against_ Wittgenstein's harness-layer framing for a well-capitalized team. It is not an argument against the harness framing for a small team, because Emu3 required training a new foundation model from scratch. But it does mean: if the LFQ tokenizer Emu3 and MAGVIT-v2 use becomes the de-facto standard, Wittgenstein's frozen-decoder bet becomes stronger, not weaker — we just inherit their artifact.

**SPAE (Google + CMU, 2023, arXiv:2306.17842).** "Semantic Pyramid AutoEncoder for Multimodal Generation with Frozen LLMs" (NeurIPS 2023). SPAE encodes images to sequences of _actual words_ from a frozen LLM's vocabulary, arranged in a semantic pyramid so the coarse tokens are semantically meaningful words and the fine tokens carry reconstruction detail. PaLM 2 and GPT-3.5 then generate images via in-context learning — no fine-tuning — by producing such word sequences, which SPAE decodes to pixels. Relevance: this is the closest prior art to Wittgenstein's exact bet. The LLM is truly frozen; the codec is a trained autoencoder; the interface is discrete tokens drawn from the LM's own vocabulary. If Wittgenstein did not know about SPAE when ADR-0005 was written, it should be treated as a direct existence proof that the approach works at all, and as a ceiling on what's achievable with a genuinely frozen LLM.

**DeepSeek-VL2 (DeepSeek-AI, Dec 2024, arXiv:2412.10302).** "Mixture-of-Experts Vision-Language Models for Advanced Multimodal Understanding." A DeepSeekMoE backbone with a dynamic-tiling high-resolution vision encoder, available in 1.0B / 2.8B / 4.5B activated-parameter variants. Reports OCRBench 834, beating GPT-4o at 736, among other understanding benchmarks. Relevance: this is the _understanding_ counterpart to the generation papers. It does not generate images — it is a standard VLM architecture (continuous vision features, MLP projector, decoder-only LM). It tells us two things. First, in April 2026, the best open understanding path is still continuous-feature + projector (LLaVA-style), not VQ tokens. Second, the understanding and generation paths are architecturally diverging: understanding wants continuous features preserved, generation wants discrete LFQ tokens. Wittgenstein should assume two codec stacks for image, not one. SEED-style unification is still aspirational.

**TA-TiTok / MaskGen (Kim et al., 2025, arXiv:2501.07730).** "Democratizing Text-to-Image Masked Generative Models with Compact Text-Aware One-Dimensional Tokens." The first named successor to TiTok: one-stage training (no two-stage distillation), supports both discrete and continuous tokens, integrates text at the de-tokenization stage. MaskGen, built on TA-TiTok, reports FID 6.53 on MJHQ-30K with 32 continuous tokens and GenEval 0.57 with 128 discrete tokens. Relevance: TA-TiTok is the 2025 state of the art on the "as few tokens as possible" axis that matters most for Wittgenstein's adapter budget. A 32-to-128 token target is inside the range where a small adapter can plausibly predict the whole image in one forward pass. This is the tokenizer to benchmark against in the next exec plan, alongside MAGVIT-v2.

## Red team

Three plausible reviewers, each answered inline.

**Diffusion-side researcher: "Why isn't diffusion the obviously right decoder for audio/video? Latent diffusion is the actual frontier. VQ is always going to trail on pure fidelity."** Correct on fidelity for unconstrained generation. Wrong for Wittgenstein's use case. Latent diffusion wants the LLM to produce a continuous latent or a text prompt, neither of which is what a text-native LLM natively emits. A text prompt works but throws away the planning-in-symbols step that is the whole point of the harness thesis — you collapse to "LLM writes a caption, diffusion draws it," which is just DALL-E 3. A continuous latent requires the LLM to emit vectors, which breaks the next-token interface. The VQ / LFQ path preserves the next-token interface end-to-end. For audio, EnCodec and SoundStream are RVQ codecs already; that is the discrete-token path, and it is winning for speech. For video, MAGVIT-v2 and Emu3 show discrete tokens are competitive. Diffusion is the right decoder if you are willing to give up the symbolic-planning interface. We are not.

**LFQ/FSQ skeptic: "If LFQ and FSQ kill codebook collapse and there's no lookup table, do we even need a 'codebook' mental model? Isn't 'VQ' the wrong word now?"** Yes, partially. The word "codebook" in ADR-0005 should be demoted to a special case. The load-bearing claim is not "a learned codebook of K centroids" — it is "a finite, discrete, integer-indexed target vocabulary that an LM output head can predict." LFQ satisfies that with no codebook. FSQ satisfies it with a grid. Classical VQ satisfies it with centroids. The correct abstraction for Wittgenstein is "discrete-token decoder family," and the current best instance is LFQ, not classical VQ. This is a real revision to the brief. See Kill criteria below.

**Emu3-native proponent: "Emu3 proved you can do video with pure next-token prediction on one model. Why are you still wrapping HyperFrames and keeping a separate text backbone?"** Because Emu3 required training a frontier model from scratch on trillions of multimodal tokens, and Wittgenstein explicitly does not. The harness thesis is a statement about which team can ship: a team that cannot train a frontier model can still ship multimodal capability by gluing a text-native frontier LLM to a frozen LFQ decoder through a small trained adapter. Emu3 is the upper bound on what a well-capitalized team can do; Wittgenstein is optimizing for a different point on the cost / capability frontier. The HyperFrame wrapping exists because our LLM is text-native and does not have image tokens in its vocabulary — Emu3's does. If and when a frontier open-weights model ships with LFQ tokens in its tokenizer, HyperFrames collapse to a nop and the harness is just the adapter. That is a feature, not a bug.

## Kill criteria

Concrete revisions that would force ADR-0005 to be rewritten.

1. **By Q4 2026, if a frontier open-weights text LLM (Llama-4, Qwen-3, DeepSeek-V4 tier) ships with native LFQ image tokens in its tokenizer and competitive image generation out of the box,** the harness framing for images collapses to identity and we should delete the adapter stage, not refine it. Probability: moderate; Chameleon and Emu3 are both moving in this direction.

2. **By Q4 2026, if a continuous-latent interface (e.g., a Perceiver-style or diffusion-transformer-style bridge) ships with published numbers beating LFQ-decoder + adapter on both FID and prompt adherence at comparable adapter parameter counts,** the discrete-token bet is wrong and we should switch the image path to continuous latents. Probability: low but nonzero; watch the MaskGen / TA-TiTok continuous-token variants.

3. **By mid-2026, if MAGVIT-v2 or a direct LFQ successor is not released under a usable license,** the "frozen pretrained decoder" bet has no artifact to point at, and we must either retrain one ourselves (breaking the "no pretraining" constraint) or fall back to VQGAN, which is now two generations stale. Probability: this is the most likely failure mode. Mitigation: identify a second-source LFQ-family open weights before Q3 2026.

4. **If, within the next two image-path iterations, the adapter cannot drive a frozen LFQ decoder to CLIPScore parity with SPAE's reported numbers on the same prompts,** the small-adapter assumption is wrong at our scale and ADR-0005 needs a larger adapter budget or a fine-tuned decoder. Probability: unknown, this is a live experimental question.

Any one of (1), (2), or (4) triggers a rewrite. (3) triggers a mitigation plan, not a rewrite.

## Verdict

The VQ / frozen-decoder bet is still right in April 2026, with one forced edit: the decoder family name is LFQ, not classical VQ. Every 2024–2025 paper that matters — Chameleon, MAGVIT-v2, Emu3, TA-TiTok — ratifies the discrete-token interface between language models and pixels; the only thing that changed is which quantization scheme sits behind that interface, and LFQ has clearly won on the merits (no codebook collapse, larger effective vocabulary, better FID under LM training). SPAE is direct prior art that the frozen-LLM version of this works at all; DeepSeek-VL2 is a reminder that understanding and generation will use different codec stacks for at least another year. The harness framing survives: Wittgenstein is not racing Emu3, it is building the version a small team can ship, and that version is "text-native LLM + trained adapter + frozen LFQ-family decoder." ADR-0005 should be revised to rename the image decoder slot from "VQ decoder" to "LFQ-family discrete-token decoder" and to name MAGVIT-v2 / TA-TiTok as the candidate artifacts. Nothing else in the image path needs to change. — research, 2026-04-23.
